What is a word frequency distribution?

A word frequency distribution is based on the word frequency table, but represents the most frequent words in a plot and not in a table. The plot can be displayed in different ways, here a one-dimensional scatter plot (also called dot plot). The x-axis of the plot represents the different features, the y-axis the frequency. In most cases, the distribution will resemble Zipf's distribution.

How to use

The word frequencies of a play or selected plays of an author are calculated here. For the latter, the checkbox Select multiple plays must be selected in the panel on the left. The word frequencies of a play/selected plays of an author are represented on the left in the form of a distribution. General options such as stop word consideration, punctuation removal and lowercase conversion of words can be selected in the left panel.

Word frequencies options

On the left panel are a number of Word frequencies options:
  • Revert the order of the frequencies: If activated, the word frequencies will be sorted in ascending order.
  • Top n frequent features: Selects how many of the top frequent features should be plotted in the distribution.
  • Select frequency method: There are two options: Relative Frequencies and Absolute Frequencies. If Absolute Frequencies is chosen, the simple counts of the words within the play will be used. If Relative Frequencies is chosen, the proportion of word frequencies in relation to the total number of words within the play or selected play will be used. Note: If multiple plays are selected, the total number of words in the combined plays is used for the Relative frequencies.
  • Use a linebreak in the title of the plot: If selected, the name of the author in the title will be moved to the next line.
  • Plot title size: The title of the plot can be enlarged or reduced with this option.

Note: For authors with multiple plays, it may take a short time to calculate all word frequencies and the respective distribution for the selected plays by that author.
