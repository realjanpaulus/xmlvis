About XMLvis

XMLvis is a tool for visualising plays in the form of XML data of the corpora of the DraCor project. It offers different visualisation methods for different questions.

XMLvis is aimed primarily at people without a technical background who want to use the DraCor corpora, e.g. for literary analyses. For this reason, XMLvis contains many beginner-friendly explanatory texts that explain the plots, the tables and their contents in detail and are intended to help with the selection of options.

But this tool can also be useful for Data Scientists, Data Analysts, Digital Humanities scholars, NLP enthusiasts and similar professions and fields from the IT-field: Should one want to use the DraCor corpus for one's research, XMLvis takes care of the first step of a typical workflow, the "insight into the corpus", without having to access external libraries. The statistics, the presentation of word frequencies as tables and distributions, the word clouds, the trend plots as well as the KWIC tables give a good first impression of the selected corpus and show its possibilities and its limitations.

It should be noted that XMLvis was only designed for a first insight or some specific questions. For more possibilities and further analysis, further tools are recommended. Voyant Tools, for example, provides even more methods for analysing corpora and making them visually accessible.
