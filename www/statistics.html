How to use

Some statistics about a play or all plays of an author are calculated here. For the latter, the checkbox "Use all plays" must be selected in the panel on the left. Options such as stop word consideration, punctuation removal and lowercase conversion of words can be selected in the left panel. Special statistics options such as the Measure for the lexical diversity option can also be found there.

The tables different statistics are now explained in more detail:

  • Overall word count of all plays (= token count): Counts all individual words (= tokens) of a play or all plays. Duplicate words are distinguished. Example: The sentence "and I waited and waited" consists of 5 tokens ("and", "I", "waited", "and", "waited").
  • Unique word count of all plays (= type count): Counts all unique words (= types) of a play or all plays. Duplicate words are not distinguished. Example: The sentence "and I waited and waited" consists of 3 types ("and", "I", "waited").
  • Proportion of stop words in the play/all plays: Proportion of stop words in the entire text (= single play or all plays). Is not influenced by the option Remove stopwords.
  • Count of sentences in the play/all plays: Count of sentences or sentence-like separation units in the entire text (= single play or all plays). This count should be considered as an approximate reference count rather than a definitive count.
  • Count of numbers in the play/all plays: Count of numeric tokens (e.g. years) in the entire text (= single play or all plays).
  • Lexical diversity in the play/all plays: Calculates the lexical diversity (or lexical richness) of the text (= single play or all plays) using the specified measure. By default, "TTR: Token-Type Ratio" is selected. This is calculated by dividing the number of types by the number of tokens. Further measures can be selected on the left using the Measure for the lexical diversity option. How these are calculated and further informations can be found in this article.
  • Text readability of the play/all plays: Calculates the readability of a text (= single play or all plays). The measure attempts to quantify how hard a text is to read. Measures can be selected on the left using the Measure for the text readability: option. The scores for the various measure differs and have their own meaning in their respective context. For example, if the popular measure "Flesch" is selected, a higher score indicates that the text is easier to read, a lower score indicates that the text is more difficult to read. Instructions on how the different measures are calculated and further informations can be found in this article.

Note: For authors with multiple plays, it may take a short time to calculate all statistics for all plays by that author.
